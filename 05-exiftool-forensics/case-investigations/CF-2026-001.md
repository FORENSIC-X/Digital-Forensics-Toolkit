# Case File: CF-2026-001
## Corporate Espionage Investigation — Metadata Analysis

**Investigating Agency:** CBI Cyber Crimes Unit (Simulated)
**Tool Used:** ExifTool 13.59
**Case Status:** Closed — Findings Documented
**Classification:** Simulated Exercise

---

## Case Background

A corporate espionage complaint was filed by a pharmaceutical firm based in Bengaluru. The firm alleged that a former employee (terminated February 1, 2026) had illegally accessed their internal R&D server post-termination and photographed confidential drug trial documents.

Three JPEG images were recovered from a shared Google Drive folder linked to an anonymous account. The firm's legal team believed the former employee had taken these photographs and uploaded them to exfiltrate confidential data.

---

## Subject's Written Statement

The subject's lawyer submitted the following written statement to investigators:

> *"My client took these photographs casually at his home on the evening of May 15, 2026, using his personal OnePlus phone. He has never been inside the firm's office after his termination on February 1, 2026. The images were taken purely for personal use and have no connection to any confidential documents."*

This statement contained four verifiable claims:

| # | Claim |
|---|---|
| 1 | Photographs were taken on May 15, 2026 |
| 2 | Device used was a personal OnePlus phone |
| 3 | Photographs were taken at his home |
| 4 | Images were not edited or processed |

---

## Evidence

Three JPEG images recovered from the anonymous Drive folder:

- `photo_001.jpg`
- `photo_002.jpg`
- `photo_003.jpg`

---

## ExifTool Command Used

```bash
exiftool -Make -Model -DateTimeOriginal -GPSLatitude -GPSLongitude -Software CF-2026-001/
```

This command extracts only the forensically critical tags from all files in the evidence folder in a single pass — device make and model, original capture timestamp, GPS coordinates, and software processing history.

## Claim-by-Claim Analysis

### Claim 1 — "Photographs taken on May 15, 2026"

**Status: Partially Collapses**

`photo_001.jpg` and `photo_002.jpg` carry a `DateTimeOriginal` of `2026:05:15` — consistent with the claimed date.

However, `photo_003.jpg` carries a `DateTimeOriginal` of `2026:03:03 09:17:22` — **30 days after the subject's termination on February 1, 2026**, and over two months before the claimed date of May 15, 2026. This photograph was not taken on May 15, 2026. The subject's statement does not account for this image.

---

### Claim 2 — "Device used was a personal OnePlus phone"

**Status: Collapses Completely**

All three images were captured on an **Apple iPhone 13 Pro** (`Make: Apple`, `Model: iPhone 13 Pro`). This directly and unambiguously contradicts the written statement. There is no OnePlus device fingerprint anywhere in the metadata.

This is not an ambiguous finding — it is a direct, verifiable falsehood in a formal legal submission to investigators.

---

### Claim 3 — "Photographs taken at his home"

**Status: Requires Further Investigation**

All three images carry identical GPS coordinates:
```
12°58'14.40"N, 77°35'22.80"E
```

These coordinates were resolved using Google Maps. The location does **not** correspond to the subject's registered home address. The coordinates resolve to a **Pharmaceutical retail outlet in Bengaluru**. 

Critically, this same GPS location appears on `photo_003.jpg`, which was taken on **March 3, 2026 at 09:17 AM** — 30 days post-termination. The subject was present at a pharmaceutical retail outlet in Bengaluru more than a month after being terminated, at the same location where all evidence images were captured.

---

### Claim 4 — "Images have not been edited or processed" (implied)

**Status: Collapses**

All three images carry `Software: Adobe Lightroom 6.0` — a professional desktop photo editing application. Every image in the evidence set was opened and processed through Lightroom after being captured. The claim of unedited personal photographs is false.

*Note: While this finding is significant, it is the weakest of the four in isolation — a defence can argue routine personal editing habits. Its strength lies in corroboration with the other findings.*

## Most Damaging Finding

The false device claim is the single most damaging finding. Raghav's lawyer submitted a written statement to the CBI specifically identifying a **OnePlus phone** as the capture device. ExifTool returns **Apple iPhone 13 Pro** on all three files without ambiguity. This is a provable lie in a formal legal submission — a serious legal problem entirely independent of the espionage charge itself, and one that immediately undermines the subject's credibility on every other claim in the statement.

---

## Formal Forensic Finding

> *Three images recovered from the anonymous Drive account were analyzed using ExifTool 13.59. Analysis contradicts the subject's written statement on multiple grounds: device make and model is Apple iPhone 13 Pro, not a OnePlus device as claimed; `photo_003.jpg` carries a `DateTimeOriginal` of `2026:03:03 09:17:22` — 30 days after the subject's stated termination date and inconsistent with the claimed capture date of May 15, 2026; all three images carry identical GPS coordinates resolving to a Pharmaceutical retail outlet in Bengaluru — not the subject's registered home address; all three images were post-processed using Adobe Lightroom 6.0, contradicting the implied claim of unedited personal photographs. The false device claim in the written legal submission materially undermines the subject's credibility across all assertions.*

## Recommended Next Steps

Based on the metadata findings, the following follow-up actions are recommended:

1. **CCTV Footage Request** — Obtain surveillance footage from the Pharmaceutical retail outlet in Bengaluru for March 3, 2026 between 09:00 AM and 10:00 AM to visually confirm the subject's presence and identify any third-party contact.

2. **Google Account Location History Subpoena** — Request the subject's Google account location history for March 3, 2026 to reconstruct the full movement timeline and confirm GPS data.

3. **Device Verification** — Obtain a warrant to seize the subject's Apple iPhone 13 Pro and verify the device's internal photo library against the recovered images using hash comparison.

4. **Third-Party Investigation** — The GPS location (a pharmaceutical retail outlet) raises the possibility of a document handoff to a third party. Identify all employees and management of that MedPlus outlet with connections to the pharmaceutical industry for further questioning.

5. **Drive Account Attribution** — Subpoena Google for the account registration details, login IP addresses, and device fingerprints associated with the anonymous Drive folder.
